Investment Thesis

HBM is Not Your Grandfather’s DRAM

Traditional DRAM / NAND

  • Highly cyclical, boom-bust pricing
  • Commodity-like behavior
  • Short qualification cycles
  • High supply elasticity
  • Easy for new entrants

HBM — Structural Shift

  • Multi-year supply agreements
  • Extremely high technical barriers (TSV + packaging)
  • Deep co-design with NVIDIA, AMD, hyperscalers
  • Very sticky once qualified
  • Capacity sold out years ahead

Key Insight: HBM margins and visibility are structurally higher. This behaves more like a high-end specialty semiconductor business than traditional memory.

The New Battleground

HBM4 — The Packaging & Process Race

HBM4 is a major leap: 2048-bit interface (double previous generation), much taller stacks (12-Hi / 16-Hi), significantly higher bandwidth, and major power efficiency gains.

Supply Dynamics

2026 HBM Supply is Extremely Tight

SK hynix

Planning major capacity expansion. Expected to maintain ~60-70% share of NVIDIA Rubin HBM4 in several analyst models.

Samsung

Resumed Pyeongtaek P5 fab. Leveraging advanced 1c DRAM to close the technology gap and regain meaningful share.

Micron

Aggressive megaplant expansion in Taiwan & Japan. Strong focus on winning design slots beyond primary NVIDIA position.

Investment Implication: Because HBM capacity is pre-sold via long-term agreements, the usual memory inventory correction risk is significantly reduced. Visibility into 2026–2027 is unusually high.
Investment Angle

What This Means for Investors

Bull Case

  • Multi-year revenue visibility from LTAs
  • High incremental margins on HBM
  • AI demand still outpacing supply additions
  • Barriers to entry continue rising
  • Potential for sustained high ROIC

Key Risks

  • Execution risk on HBM4 yields & qualification
  • High customer concentration (NVIDIA)
  • Very high ongoing capex intensity
  • Geopolitical risk (Korea / Taiwan / export controls)
  • Potential future oversupply if all expansions succeed
Relative Positioning (mid-2026):

Samsung — Most undervalued optionality if HBM4 execution succeeds.
Conclusion

Key Takeaways — June 2026

01
HBM is structurally different — long-term contracts and extreme technical barriers create a more stable, higher-margin business than traditional memory.

SK hynix currently leads on technology, share, and customer intimacy. Hard to displace in the near term.
03
Micron is the fastest riser and a credible #2. Strong torque for investors betting on share gains.

Samsung has the most upside optionality if its 1c + advanced packaging execution delivers in HBM4.

China remains a long-term strategic concern but is not yet a material competitor in high-performance HBM.
