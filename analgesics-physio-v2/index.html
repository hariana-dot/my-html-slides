🎯 Key Takeaways

1. Match drug to pain mechanism — Inflammatory → NSAIDs. Neuropathic → Adjuvants. This is the single most important clinical skill.

2. Work with the medication timeline — Schedule your interventions during the drug's peak effect window. Document response.

3. Always screen for red flags — GI, renal, CV, hepatic comorbidities change the risk–benefit profile of every drug.

4. Topicals are underused — First-line for localised OA (NICE). Zero systemic side effects. Recommend them confidently.

5. Opioids have a role, but respect the risk — Appropriate for acute severe pain. Avoid for chronic non-cancer pain. Monitor for red flags.

6. You are the bridge to the prescriber — You see patients more frequently than GPs. Your feedback on their medication response is clinically valuable.
