Scoring System

General Scoring Guidelines

Scores 1–3 indicate deficiencies; scores 4 and 5 define competence levels.

Score 1

Significant Deficiencies

Documentation or clinical reasoning deficiencies that may compromise patient safety or care quality.

Score 2

Multiple Deficiencies

Indicates a need for substantial improvement across several areas of practice.

Score 3

Minor Deficiencies

Inconsistencies requiring attention to detail and adherence to standard practices.

Pass Threshold

Score ≥ 4 in ALL seven areas. Feedback is constructive, concise, and professional. For scores of 4, qualitative feedback highlights where elements of a 5 are partially achieved, without introducing fractional scores.

Area 1 of 7

1Documentation Quality

Evaluating completeness, clarity, and organization of clinical records.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Complete & Compliant

  • All mandatory information is present
  • Required documents (assessment forms, consent) are uploaded
  • Adheres to organizational standards for completeness
Score 5 — Experienced

Clear, Concise & Well-Organized

  • Exceptionally clear and concise documentation
  • Well-organized for immediate understanding of patient status, progress, and rationale
  • Includes relevant contextual details without being verbose

Key distinction: Score 5 goes beyond mere compliance—documentation that facilitates immediate understanding of the full clinical picture.

Area 2 of 7

2Clinical Reasoning: Nature & Severity

Assessing diagnostic accuracy and depth of condition analysis.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Accurate Identification

  • Diagnosis and primary conditions accurately identified and documented
  • Clear statement of severity or stage (e.g., "Acute LBP, moderate severity")
  • Reasoning follows a logical, evidence-informed process
Score 5 — Experienced

Sophisticated Clinical Reasoning

  • Links diagnosis and severity to specific functional limitations and participation restrictions
  • Considers relevant prognostic factors
  • Addresses intrinsic and extrinsic factors (comorbidities, social determinants of health) influencing condition and recovery
Area 3 of 7

3Clinical Reasoning: Personal & Social History

Evaluating how personal and social context informs clinical decision-making.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Accurate & Complete Documentation

  • Pertinent history elements documented (living situation, support systems, prior functional status)
  • Directly relevant to the patient's current condition
Score 5 — Experienced

Holistic Integration

  • Integrates history into a holistic understanding of the patient
  • Demonstrates how factors influence health beliefs, coping strategies, and therapy engagement
  • Uses information to tailor interventions and anticipate potential barriers
Area 4 of 7

4Clinical Reasoning: Client's Perspective

Assessing patient-centered care through documentation of subjective experience.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Active Listening Documented

  • Client's reported symptoms, concerns, and expectations accurately documented
  • Acknowledges the patient's subjective experience
Score 5 — Experienced

Shared Decision-Making

  • Emphathetic understanding of the client's perspective demonstrated
  • Incorporates patient values and preferences into shared decision-making
  • Notes reflect how symptoms impact daily life
  • Client's perspective guides goal setting and intervention choices
Area 5 of 7

5Clinical Reasoning: Use of Objective Measures

Evaluating selection, application, and interpretation of standardized measures.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Appropriate & Accurate

  • Uses standardized, valid, and reliable measures (MFAC, EMS, goniometry, MMT)
  • Measures applied correctly
  • Results recorded accurately
Score 5 — Experienced

Strategic Selection & Interpretation

  • Demonstrates understanding of psychometric properties and clinical utility
  • Utilizes broader range when indicated (single leg standing, balance tests, walking speed, diagnosis-specific measures)
  • Compares current findings to previous sessions to track progress and inform modifications
Area 6 of 7

6Clinical Reasoning: Problem Identification & Prioritization

Assessing the ability to synthesize and prioritize patient problems.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Clear Definition & Alignment

  • Key problems identified and prioritized accurately based on assessment findings
  • Aligned with standard clinical practice
  • Problems clearly defined and directly address functional limitations and impairments
Score 5 — Experienced

Advanced Synthesis

  • Identifies underlying causes and interrelationships between problems
  • Prioritizes by clinical urgency AND patient-centered goals
  • Considers likelihood of successful intervention
  • Accounts for intrinsic and extrinsic factors (motivation, environmental barriers)
Area 7 of 7

7Clinical Reasoning: Treatment Plans & Goals

Evaluating goal-setting and intervention planning quality.

Score 4 — Entry-Level / Safe

Logical & Patient-Centered

  • Logical, patient-centered, and measurable goals (SMART goals)
  • Goals directly linked to identified problems and assessment findings
  • Treatment plan aligns with clinical guidelines and active case list requirements (A, B, C, PRN)
Score 5 — Experienced

Individualized & Dynamic

  • Highly individualized plans reflecting unique presentation, prognosis, and psychosocial context
  • Goals ambitious yet realistic
  • Demonstrates adaptability based on ongoing patient response, condition changes, and goal progress
  • Reflects consideration of motivation, environmental factors, and updated chief complaints
  • Explicitly considers frequency and duration expectations of the assigned active case list

Overall Assessment

Pass or Needs Improvement?

A therapist must achieve at least Score 4 in all seven areas to receive an overall Pass.

✓

Pass

Score ≥ 4 in every domain. Feedback is constructive and highlights strengths.

⚠

To Be Improved

Any domain scored ≤ 3. Targeted feedback identifies areas for development.

Score 4 feedback highlights elements of a 5 that are partially achieved. No fractional scores are used. Every review is constructive, concise, and professional.
