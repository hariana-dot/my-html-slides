Investment Thesis

HBM is Not Your Grandfather’s DRAM

Traditional DRAM / NAND

  • Highly cyclical, boom-bust pricing
  • Commodity-like, price sensitive
  • Short qualification cycles
  • High supply elasticity
  • Easy for new entrants to ramp

HBM — Structural Shift

  • Long-term supply agreements (multi-year, volume committed)
  • Extremely high technical barriers (TSV, packaging, yield)
  • Deep co-design with customers (NVIDIA, AMD, hyperscalers)
  • Sticky relationships — once qualified, hard to displace
  • Capacity sold out years in advance

Key Insight: HBM margins and visibility are structurally higher. This is closer to a specialty foundry business than traditional memory.

Supply Dynamics

2026 HBM Supply is Extremely Tight

SK hynix

Planning major capacity expansion. KRW 40T+ capex range discussed for memory. HBM4 share expected to remain dominant (~60-70% for NVIDIA Rubin in some analyst estimates).

Samsung

Resumed Pyeongtaek P5 fab construction. Leveraging advanced 1c DRAM to close technology gap. Targeting meaningful share recovery in HBM4.

Micron

Aggressive “megaplant” expansion in Taiwan and Japan. Strong focus on winning design slots beyond primary NVIDIA position. High capex intensity.

Investment Implication: Because HBM capacity is pre-sold via long-term agreements, the usual memory inventory correction risk is significantly reduced. Visibility into 2026–2027 revenue is unusually high for all three players.
Investment Angle

What This Means for Investors

Bull Case for the Group

  • Multi-year visibility from LTAs (long-term agreements)
  • High incremental margins on HBM vs legacy DRAM
  • AI demand growth still outpacing supply additions
  • Barriers to entry rising (not falling)
  • Potential for sustained high ROIC in HBM segment

Key Risks & Watch Points

  • Execution risk on HBM4 yields and qualification
  • Customer concentration (especially NVIDIA exposure)
  • Capex intensity remains very high
  • Geopolitical risk (Korea, Taiwan, US export controls)
  • Potential future oversupply if all expansions succeed simultaneously
Relative Positioning (as of mid-2026):
SK hynix — Highest quality compounder with best visibility.
Micron — Highest torque to share gains; more cyclical but faster upside.
Samsung — Most undervalued optionality if HBM4 execution succeeds.
Conclusion

Key Takeaways — June 2026

01
HBM is structurally different — long-term contracts and extreme technical barriers create a more stable, higher-margin business than traditional memory.
02
SK hynix currently leads on technology, share, and customer intimacy. Hard to displace in the near term.
03
Micron is the fastest riser and a credible #2. Strong torque for investors betting on share gains.
04
Samsung has the most upside optionality if its 1c + advanced packaging execution delivers in HBM4.
05
China remains a long-term strategic concern but is not yet a material competitor in high-performance HBM.

All three companies are well positioned for the multi-year AI infrastructure buildout. The winners will be those who execute flawlessly on HBM4 yields, capacity, and customer qualifications.
